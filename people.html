Principal Investigator

doc. Mgr. Petr Dvořák, Ph.D.

Associate Proffesor

Chairman of the Czech Phycological Society

Associate Proffesor

I am an associate professor at the Department of Botany, Palacký University Olomouc (Czech Republic) and chairman of the Czech Phycological Society. I was a postdoc at Uppsala University (Sweden) and completed habilitation at Charles University, Prague (Czech Republic). I am interested in evolutionary biology, taxonomy, and systematics of cyanobacteria and algae. Currently, I am investigating factors driving the emergence of new species in Microcoleus (cyanobacteria) and chrysophytes (Synura and Mallomonas) using population genomics, phylogenomics, and bioinformatics. I also pursue the application of phylogenomics and population genomics into the taxonomy across all cyanobacterial groups.

Researchers

Aleksandar Stanojković

I am an evolutionary biologist interested in genomics of microorganisms, especially cyanobacteria. I completed my PhD at Palacký University Olomouc in 2023, where I investigated evolutionary forces governing diversification of cyanobacteria on a global and local scale by focusing on genome-wide signatures of diversity and divergence. I am a postdoctoral researcher in the same lab since October 2023. I am currently applying bioinfomatics approaches to study the adaptation and genetic variation of Microcoleus cyanobacterium using nanopore sequencing data.

Svatopluk Skoupý

I am a molecular biologist, interested in population genomics and taxonomy of micoralgae and specifically cyanobacteria. I am currently finishing my PhD studies at Palacký University in Olomouc, focusing on investigating population genomics and biogeography of fillamentous soil-crust forming cyanobacteria, with the employment of large-scale datasets.
